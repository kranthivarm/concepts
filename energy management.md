## Question 1: What are the activities you do that make you relax (Calm quadrant)?

These are the things that genuinely help me slow down:
- Listening to music  
- Going for a walk in a green area  
- Sitting quietly and focusing on my breath  
- Reading something light and enjoyable  
- Lying down without using my phone  

---

## Question 2: When do you find yourself getting into the Stress quadrant?

I usually feel stressed when:
- I’m learning something new and struggling  
- Deadlines or exams are close  
- Code isn’t working and I’m stuck  
- I have to speak in front of people without having enough knowledge  

---

## Question 3: How do you know when you are in the Excitement quadrant?

I know I’m in a good state when:
- I feel curious and energized  
- I lose track of time  
- The task feels challenging but doable  
- I’m focused without forcing it  
- I want to continue working  

---

## Question 4: Key ideas about sleep

- Sleep helps in learning  
- Helps with focus and emotional balance  
- Supports physical recovery  
- Reduces stress and anxiety  
- Protects long-term brain health  

---

## Question 5: What are some ideas you can implement to sleep better?

Things I can actually follow:
- Sleep and wake up at the same time  
- Avoiding blue screens before sleep  
- Avoid heavy meals at night  
- Reduce caffeine consumption in the evening  
- Keep the room dark and silent  

---

## Question 6: Key ideas about how exercise helps the brain

- Improves learning and focus  
- Reduces stress  
- Boosts mood naturally  
- Keeps the brain flexible  
- Improves quality of sleep  

---

## Question 7: What are some steps you can take to exercise more?

Simple steps I can maintain:
- Walk every day  
- Stretch lightly in the morning  
- Use stairs when possible  
- Move during breaks  
- Play a sport on weekends
